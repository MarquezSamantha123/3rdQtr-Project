March is Fire Prevention Month in the Philippines, when the temperature and humidity are at their highest. These circumstances frequently result in fires. 13,029 fire occurrences were reported by the Bureau of Fire Protection in the entire nation in 2022. Due to the alarmingly high number of fire-related cases that occur primarily in March, then-Philippine President Ferdinand E. Marcos proclaimed March to be Fire Prevention Month in Presidential Proclamation No. 115-A, which was enacted into law in 1966.





How to Prevent a Fire in your Home
While cooking, never leave the kitchen unattended. When you are cooking, you should never leave it as you might forget it and it may lead to a fire if left for too long.
Any combustible items should be stored safely. We should store them safely because they are capable of catching on fire when not being careful around them and to store them safely they must be sealed tightly and must be in a ventilated place.
Keep your cables clean. Cables/Wires should be organized as it will make it a safer environment and that it will prevent people from incidents like getting electrocuted, falling over and/or getting strangled
Have a blanket, fire extinguisher or fire hose reel nearby at all times. Fire extinguishers should be used when you can't get close to the fire. Fire blanket should be used when there are fires like clothing fire and small kitchen fires. The fire hose reel should be used to keep a fire hose that can be used for either indoor or outdoor fires.
